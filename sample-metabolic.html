Nutrition · The reasoning

Why the plan is built this way.

Three evidence-based levers drive this plan: combined training, lean-mass-first eating, and carbohydrate quantity over grain type. The science is below in plain terms — your labs, bloodwork, supplements, and any medication stay with your physician (see the note at the end).

Training

Combined > either alone

Resistance + aerobic together is the only combination that reliably moves HbA1c, and it reduces visceral fat far more than either modality alone. VO₂max is the highest-yield single metric — which is why HIIT is non-negotiable, not optional.

Frame

Lean-mass first

Skeletal muscle is a primary site of glucose disposal, so building and protecting it raises insulin sensitivity directly. Protein stays high (~1.8–2.0 g/kg) and spread across the day so muscle protein synthesis stays switched on through a busy shift schedule.

Carbs

Quantity, then quality

The biggest dietary lever is total carbohydrate amount, not just swapping refined for whole grains. We moderate total carbs, cluster most of them around training, and lean Mediterranean — high fiber, fish and plants, olive oil over ghee.

GLUT4 translocation and insulin sensitivity are highest around training, so that's where carbohydrate earns its place. Evening/overnight carbohydrate is handled less well — keep night-shift intake light. Protein stays steady all week.

Heart-smart swaps

  • White rice (2–3 cups) → ½ cup brown rice / cauliflower rice
  • Maida naan / multiple rotis → 1–2 whole-grain or bajra roti
  • Ghee / seed oils → extra-virgin olive or avocado oil
  • Sugar in chai → unsweetened or minimal
  • Samosa / pakora → roasted chana, nuts
  • Low-protein meals → double the dal, add paneer / eggs / chicken

Low-friction shift prep

  • Pre-shift bowl (~4 min)Rotisserie chicken + ½ cup brown rice or quinoa + a big handful of spinach + a drizzle of EVOO.
  • Batch proteinGround turkey 93/7 with whatever spices you like, cooked on an off day; assemble with cauliflower rice through a run of night shifts.
  • Locker stockHigh-protein shakes, Greek yogurt, raw almonds — so 3 a.m. decisions aren't willpower decisions.
  • Family mealsAsk for your portion to be pulled before the final ghee tadka, or request olive/avocado oil.
Quick reference · the day

Eating around the rotation — the physiology

  • Front-load the active windowGlucose tolerance tracks the circadian clock — higher earlier in your waking day, lower toward biological night — so the same meal is handled better earlier than late. Build the day's calories into your awake-and-working hours.
  • Pre-shift, ~2–3 h before the floorProtein + low-glycemic carbohydrate. Steadier glucose across a long shift; the protein blunts the post-meal rise and primes muscle protein synthesis.
  • Protein, evenly spread~40–50 g at each of 3–4 meals. Each dose clears the leucine threshold and triggers a fresh MPS pulse — more effective than one large serving, and key while building lean mass on a hard schedule.
  • On the night-shift blockLean on time-restricted eating: largest meal pre-shift, then keep overnight intake light (one low-GI snack before ~1 a.m. if needed). Large meals in the biological night blunt next-morning glucose tolerance.
  • Caffeine + sleepCaffeine's ~5 h half-life means a 4 a.m. coffee still blocks adenosine at 8 a.m. and wrecks day-sleep. Set a hard caffeine curfew ~8 h before your target sleep, and a 100 oz/24 h hydration floor with electrolytes mid-shift.
